## Emulation/Verification Screening + Interview Prep

**What is Emulation, as opposed to Verification?**

Emulation is using FPGA boards to *emulate* the functionality of an SoC currently in design. It is part of verification in the industry's push towards moving their testing strategy to the left. In other words, verifying functionality of expensive designs well before they are manufactured and received from the fab. 

Verification relies on using software models to verify blocks within an IP. It's more low-level than Emulation. As mentioned above, Emulation is the final step in the chip verification/validation process, where the RTL code is ported to a hardware platform, like an FPGA, and verified wholly.
