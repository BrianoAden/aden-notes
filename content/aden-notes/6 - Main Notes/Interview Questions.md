**What work did you do for GDMS over the Summer?**

I was a Systems Engineering Intern with GDMS over the Summer. Since we're well out of the Systems Model and Requirements development part of the program, I spent a lot of time in the lab shadowing some of the other Systems Engineers on the team during their module testing. This gave me a lot of good experience working with lab equipment (DMMs, Oscilloscopes, etc) and a lot of hardware troubleshooting experience.

I was fairly integrated into the team, so many of my tasks were centered around prepping for unit integration. After sufficient time shadowing, I was given my own module to test. There ended up being a few issues with it, which is typical of module testing. I ended up resolving a CAN communication error, fully prepping the module for unit integration later on down the line. If the module had been pushed to integration, which from what I hear is typical of Digital control modules, it would have cost GDMS at least a few weeks to troubleshoot the issue and send it off to rework. This would have been particularly difficult considering the system would likely not be as isolated as it were during module test, making pinpointing the issue much more time-consuming. 

**How do you balance your time when you have many things to do?**

I like to proportion my time. If I have a lot of things to do, I prefer to work on things that require my attention the most. This could be items that are objectively more difficult, or have a strict deadline, or others are dependent on the completion of this task to continue with their own work. I primarily rely on tools like google calendar, to-do lists, weekly recalibrations where I look at where most of my time has gone and if anything needs to be readjusted for the following week. 

But I also believe in work-life balance, which seems to be very important at GDMS and something that I admire about the company. I personally find that if I don't give myself time to relax, I won't perform very well in my work.

**Why do you want to pursue a Master's degree?**

**What leadership experience do you have?**

I'm currently the Electrical Lead for Rice Robotics Rover team. This mainly involves high level system design, so breaking up our large Rover system into various subsystems, identifying desired functionality, how we plan on testing this functionality in relevant isolated subsystems. 

It involves coming up with plans for integration and testing the integrated unit. I also come up with learning outcomes for each subsystem so that new members who aren't quite sure where they might fit can easily determine which project meets their desired learning goals. 

But I'm also a technical lead. So I still get down to the low-level aspects of design and try to help out where I can. 

Outside of Robotics, I'm also a leader in the classroom. I've tutored countless students at Rice enrolled in Calculus I/II, I was a program fellow for a Rice Summer bridge program, where I taught Physics and Calculus and arranged social events for the students. 

I'm also a TA for the Digital Logic Design course at my University. I host Office Hours and review sessions for my students to ask questions and learn the concepts with my guidance.
