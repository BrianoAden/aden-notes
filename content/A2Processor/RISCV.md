# What is RISC-V?  

RISC-V is an open-source instruction set architecture, or [[ISA]], used to design low-power [[Processors|processors]]for a variety of applications, from [[EmbeddedSystems|embedded]] to computing devices.  

RISC processors were originally developed by the Parallel Computing Lab at the University of California, Berkeley, with RISC-V being the fifth generation processor built upon the reduced instruction set computer, or [[RISC]], concept. Before the advent of RISC-V, complex instruction set computers, or [[CISC]] computers, were the main ISA being developed and sold on the market. Nowadays, both ISAs have settled into their niche in the market, with CISC primarily being developed for desktop computers and HPC applications for its backwards compatibility and efficiency at handling complex workloads, and RISC primarily dominating the mobile device and low-power embedded side of the market.
