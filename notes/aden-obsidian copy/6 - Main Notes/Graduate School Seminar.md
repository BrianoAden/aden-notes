# PhD

Apply to faculty and labs, not school.
Reach out to faculty and grad students. Ask about their experience at the school, things they like, things they don't like. 

Look into 2-3 professors per school.  Apply to fellowships. They provide you with your own funding, which makes it easier for you to pursue research in an area that you're interested in. Also important considering the cuts in academic funding.

If research experience doesn't match with your PhD interests, apply to other schools and do research there in the Summer. Research is very important (advice N/A to Seniors).

Stick to faculty for LOR for PhD. Exceptions for internships in industry research labs. This also applies to thesis Master's programs. It's okay to get LOR from professor's who you have only taken classes with. It is best to get LOR from research advisors, though.
# General Tips

Get first essay done as quick as possible. Polish it and use it to apply to other schools. Mention professors you're interested in in your essay so that you have a larger chance to be accepted into their lab.

Graduate coursework doesn't make or break your application. A low course load might point towards lack of ambition in your application, however. 

Make sure to tell your LOR that you would require something from them ASAP. Remind them often. 

For Professional Master's programs, LOR from industry are okay. 

Even though you apply middle of first semester, graduate programs typically ask for transcripts after the semester is over. Keep your grades up. Some applications are due after the semester is over, too.

A final transcript is also required once you join as a Graduate student. They will *probably not* rescind an acceptance if you do poorly in your last semester, but it's good to keep them up regardless.
